TIE-BREAKING RULES:
	1	If a specific diagnosis is stated in the indication or diagnosis field, use that over background history.
	2	If multiple historical diagnoses are mentioned, choose the one clearly linked to the need for shunting.
	3	If a patient has tumour history but the note says the shunt is for NPH or IIH, choose NPH or IIH.
	4	If a patient has previous infection or haemorrhage but the note does not link it to hydrocephalus/shunting, do not choose it.
	5	If the only clear information is "post-haemorrhagic hydrocephalus", choose the most specific haemorrhage type if stated; otherwise choose the closest other non-traumatic haemorrhage/SAH/unknown vascular option.
	6	If the only clear information is "post-infective hydrocephalus", choose the specific infection if stated; otherwise choose "Other infection" if available.
	7	If the only clear information is "communicating hydrocephalus" or "obstructive hydrocephalus" without a cause, choose "Unknown" unless a matching specific cause is documented.
